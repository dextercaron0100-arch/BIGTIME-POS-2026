BIGTIME POS

Accomplished Standard Functional and Technical Requirements Form (Annex "B")

Prepared on: April 14, 2026

System name: BIGTIME POS

System coverage: Dashboard, central API, and Android/Windows POS terminal application

Document status: Internal accomplished working draft for Annex "B" review

Purpose

This document is a working draft of the Standard Functional and Technical Requirements form (Annex "B") for BIGTIME POS. It is based on:

  • the official BIR Annex "B" checklist
  • the BIR accreditation framework under RMO No. 24-2023
  • the current BIGTIME POS codebase, system documentation, and observable application behavior as of April 14, 2026

This draft is intended to help prepare the final sworn statement package, system demonstration, and supporting evidence bundle. Final sign-off should still be based on:

  • deployed production configuration
  • actual printed receipts and reports
  • final BIR settings per branch/terminal
  • documentary and operational controls outside the software itself

Legend

  • YES - feature is implemented and demonstrable in the current system
  • PARTIAL - feature is present in part, is configuration-dependent, or still needs final template/policy validation
  • NO - feature is not yet verified or not implemented in the current build
  • N/A - not applicable to the present scope of BIGTIME POS as a POS and back-office platform

1. System-Generated Receipts / Invoices

DescriptionCompliedRemarks
a. Seller's Registered NameYESReceipt header supports registered/store name pulled from branch and BIR settings.
b. Seller's Business Name / Business StyleYESStore name and proprietor/business identifiers are supported in the receipt header configuration.
c. Seller's Registered Address where machine will be usedYESBusiness address lines are supported in receipt header settings.
d. VAT REG TIN or NON-VAT REG TIN followed by TIN and branch codePARTIALThe invoice header supports the seller TIN line. Exact nine-digit TIN plus five-digit branch code formatting still depends on final branch-level BIR configuration.
e. Serial Number with minimum six running digits and numbering conventionYESSales invoice numbering is server-issued and normalized to the SI 000001 style with zero-padded six-digit presentation.
f. Date of TransactionYESReceipt output includes transaction date and time.
g. Space for Customer/Buyer details: registered name, address, TIN, branch code, business styleYESThe payment flow now supports buyer name, address, TIN/branch code entry, and business style, and the printed sales invoice shows the captured buyer information when provided.
h. QuantityYESQuantity is printed for each sold line item.
i. Description of items sold / nature of servicesYESItem description is printed in the receipt body.
j. Unit Cost / SRPYESUnit price is printed per line item.
k. Total Cost / AmountYESLine totals and total amount are generated and printed.
l. VAT AmountYESVAT amount is reflected for taxable transactions.
m. Mixed transaction breakdown: VATable, VAT, VAT Exempt, Zero-RatedYESReceipt computation and print layout now support VATable, VAT, VAT-exempt, and zero-rated breakdown lines based on the stored VAT type of each sold line item.
n. Word EXEMPT shown for exempt-only transactionsYESExempt-only invoice output now prints an EXEMPT marker when the sale is entirely VAT-exempt.
o. SC/PWD details: TIN, ID no., discount breakdown, signature spacePARTIALSC and PWD discounts are now quick-selectable from the cashier discount dialog (SC 20% / PWD 20%). The printed receipt now labels the discount line as SC DISCOUNT or PWD DISCOUNT and includes blank ID NO., SIGNATURE, and PRINTED NAME lines. Senior or PWD TIN capture and the statutory VAT-exempt base computation still require final configuration and validation.
p. Bottom print details: acknowledgment certificate, date issued, series range, five-year validity phrase, input tax phrasePARTIALPermit number, MIN, serial number, permit date issued, ATP number/date issued, and approved serial range are now configurable and printable. Final taxpayer-specific footer wording should still be checked against the latest submission copy and branch registration data before sign-off.
q. Word REPRINT on duplicate or subsequent printoutsYESReprint flow applies copy labeling and requires a reprint reason.

3. System-Generated Books of Accounts

DescriptionCompliedRemarks
a. General Journal mandatory fields under RR No. 9-2009PARTIALLedger and reporting foundations exist, but the final General Journal output format should still be validated against the RR No. 9-2009 field layout.
b. General Ledger mandatory fields under RR No. 9-2009PARTIALThe system stores transaction-ledger data, but final General Ledger presentation and export layout still require Annex "B" format validation.
c. Sales Journal mandatory fields under RR No. 9-2009PARTIALSales, OR/reference, discounts, VAT, and totals are available. Buyer-specific fields depend on invoice-mode data capture and final report template validation.
d. Purchase Journal mandatory fields under RR No. 9-2009PARTIALSupplier, purchase-order, and inventory modules exist in the back office, but final Purchase Journal output validation is still needed.
e. Inventory Book mandatory fields under RR No. 9-2009PARTIALInventory and stock modules are available, but the final Inventory Book report/export format should still be standardized for Annex "B".

4. Printout and Electronic Copy of Reports

DescriptionCompliedRemarks
a. Taxpayer's Registered Name on reportsYESDashboard receipt-register exports now print the registered name in the BIR metadata header sourced from branch BIR settings.
b. Registered Address where such reports are generatedYESDashboard receipt-register exports now include the registered business address in the BIR metadata header.
c. VAT REG TIN or NON-VAT REG TIN with seller TIN and branch codeYESAll dashboard CSV exports now include a TIN row formatted as VAT REG TIN {tin} using the BIR Settings value, consistent across the Sales Invoice Register, Sales Summary, and BIR E-Sales exports.
d. Software Name and Version NumberYESAll dashboard CSV exports now include Software Name: BIGTIME POS and Software Version: 1.0.0 rows in the metadata header. The thermal receipt footer now also prints SOFTWARE: BIGTIME POS v1.0.0.
e. User Name / ID of the one who generated the reportYESAll dashboard CSV exports now include a Generated By row showing the logged-in user's name and employee code from the active session.
f. Date and Time Stamp when report was generatedYESDashboard receipt-register exports already include the generation date and time in the report metadata header.

DescriptionCompliedRemarks
The system generates an audit trail or activity log of transactions and activities that can be printedYESBIGTIME POS maintains hash-linked audit and receipt-event logs. 10. Complete Audit Trail Functionalities

DescriptionCompliedRemarks
a. System controls the receipt numberingYESOfficial OR numbering is server-issued and centrally controlled by the POS ledger service.
b. Posting date of receipt transaction is the date it entered the systemYESTransactions are stored with persistent created/posting timestamps at the time of entry.
c. Transaction can be voided, but not modified once postedYESPosted sales are preserved in append-only fashion. Adjustments use controlled void/refund workflows instead of direct record editing.
d. Run date is the date the report was generatedYESSales summaries, end-of-day reports, and related outputs carry generation timestamps.
e. Users are prevented from editing data within system-generated reportsPARTIALEnd users do not directly edit generated outputs in the current flow, but readonly export controls should still be validated in the final dashboard report package.
f. Users are prevented from having the capability to override edits within computer programsYESApplication actions are role-protected and critical transaction records are stored through guarded service flows rather than ad hoc direct editing.
g. Each transaction / record is stamped with the user ID of the individual that created the transactionYESCashier IDs and user IDs are stored in transactions, sessions, and audit entries.
h. System totals and cross-checks totals and reports incorrect computation or out-of-balance conditionYESThe system computes subtotal, VAT, discounts, tendered amount, change, sales summaries, and Z-readings programmatically.

11. System Security Framework

DescriptionCompliedRemarks
a. Standard procedure and approval in securing user accessPARTIALAuthenticated access, roles, and account provisioning exist. Formal written provisioning/approval SOP should still be attached as an operational control.
b. Users are restricted from being active on multiple terminals at the same time with the same identification codeYESActive session tracking revokes prior active sessions for the same user upon new login.
c. User identification codes are deactivated after unsuccessful attempts to sign onYESLogin security now tracks failed attempts per user code and temporarily deactivates sign-in after repeated failures until the lockout window expires.
d. Password is required to be changed every thirty (30) daysYESThe authentication flow now enforces thirty-day PIN rotation and requires a successful PIN change before dashboard or POS access continues when the credential is expired or reset.
e. Non-admin users are prevented from accessing sensitive operating system commandsPARTIALApplication roles block sensitive business actions, but OS-level workstation lockdown depends on device hardening and operational setup.
f. Passwords require a combination of alphabetic and numeric charactersPARTIALWeb/admin access can support stronger credentials, but the cashier terminal presently uses PIN-based authentication instead of an alphanumeric-only policy.
g. Users are prevented from accessing accounting information except through authorized transactionsYESProtected resources are guarded by JWT authentication, throttling, and role-based authorization.
h. User access rights can be eliminated or revised upon termination or transferPARTIALEmployee/user administration and inactive-account handling exist, but HR offboarding and reassignment remain operational processes.
i. Computerized audit trails are protected from modification and destructionYESCritical audit records are hash-chained, and destructive reset operations are disabled in compliance mode.
j. Server rooms and data centers are locked and access-controlledPARTIALThis requirement depends on the selected hosting facility or on-premise deployment controls rather than code alone.
k. Server rooms and data centers are equipped with air-conditioningPARTIALEnvironmental control is a deployment-site requirement outside the application code.
l. Server rooms and data centers are protected against power failure and firePARTIALThis is dependent on UPS, fire-suppression, and facility controls in the production environment.
m. Server room and data center cables are properly installedPARTIALCabling standard is a physical infrastructure requirement to be satisfied by the deployment environment.
n. Unused network connection points are disabledPARTIALNetwork hardening is handled at the infrastructure layer and should be addressed during deployment.
o. All incoming dial-up uses strong password authenticationN/ADial-up connectivity is not part of the BIGTIME POS architecture.
p. Remote access via Internet is encryptedPARTIALProduction remote access can be protected with HTTPS/TLS and secure network configuration, but this must be enforced in deployment.
q. Access to external networks occurs over a firewall and is monitored and auditedPARTIALFirewalling and monitoring depend on the final hosting/network stack and should be documented operationally.
r. Application access is protected with an authentication mechanismYESApplication login, JWT/session handling, and role-based access are implemented.
s. Database record modification is logged for critical applicationsPARTIALCritical sales, receipt, shift, and audit events are logged. Full database-wide change logging for every non-critical master table is not yet being claimed in this draft.

12. Adjustment to Sales / Revenue

DescriptionCompliedRemarks
a. There is a mechanism that enables adjustment to sales / revenue, such as supplementary documents generated and issued to other partiesPARTIALBIGTIME POS supports void and refund workflows with reason capture and audit logging. Final supplementary document templates and business process notes should still be standardized.
b. Supplementary documents must have a unique serial numberYESVoid events now carry a unique voidRef in the format VOID-{BRANCH}-{TIMESTAMP} assigned at the time of voiding. Refund transactions already carried their own sequential OR number and a REFUND-{BRANCH}-{TIMESTAMP} reference. Both are stored in the ledger and included in audit entries.

Overall Assessment

BIGTIME POS already demonstrates a strong compliance foundation for the Annex "B" checklist in the following areas:

  • controlled receipt issuance and server-side OR handling
  • sales, VAT, discount, and payment computation
  • audit trail and receipt-event logging with downloadable BIR-headered CSV export
  • void/refund adjustment workflows with unique void reference (VOID-{BRANCH}-{TIMESTAMP}) and refund OR numbers
  • Z-reading and BIR/EIS monitoring capabilities
  • authenticated access with session tracking and role-based protection
  • central API plus local POS storage for operational continuity
  • software name and version on all CSV exports and printed receipts
  • generated-by user attribution (name and employee code) on all CSV exports
  • SC/PWD discount labeling (SC DISCOUNT / PWD DISCOUNT) with signature and ID fields on receipt

The main items still requiring final validation, policy attachment, or template refinement are:

  • SC/PWD statutory invoice layout fully complete (TIN and OSCA/PWD card number capture still needed)
  • final Books of Accounts and full report export templates beyond the current receipt register
  • long-term backup retention policy and hosting controls
  • final written SOP attachments for provisioning, approval, and periodic security review
  • physical infrastructure controls outside the application code
